Dear Class 10 Student & Parent 👋

Exam stress is completely normal — and CBSE has officially recognised it. This kit is built 100% from CBSE's own stress-management resources so you can face the 2026 boards with clarity, calm, and confidence.

📌 CBSE's Official 5 A's to Control Exam Stress
1
Acknowledge Accept that stress is natural. Every student feels it — you're not alone.
2
Appreciate Focus on what you have already prepared instead of what is left.
3
Alleviate Use relaxation techniques — deep breathing, short walks, short breaks.
4
Alter Change negative thoughts into realistic ones. Rewrite the story in your head.
5
Avoid Avoid last-minute cramming and comparison with others.
🧘 5 Instant Relief Techniques (CBSE Recommended)
1
Deep Breathing (5 seconds)
Shut your eyes → Inhale for 4 counts → Hold 4 → Exhale for 6. Repeat 5 times. CBSE calls this "the quickest and most effective way."
2
Progressive Muscle Relaxation
Tighten and release each muscle group (toes → face) for 5 seconds each. Reduces physical tension instantly.
3
4-7-8 Breathing
Inhale for 4 seconds → Hold breath for 7 seconds → Exhale slowly for 8 seconds. CBSE-endorsed relaxation technique.
4
Positive Self-Talk
Replace "I will fail" with "I am prepared and I will do my best." Your words shape your mindset.
📅 7-Day Pre-Exam Daily Routine (CBSE Approved)
🌅
Sleep 7–8 hours every night — your brain consolidates memory during sleep!
🥗
Eat balanced meals and stay well hydrated throughout the day.
🚶
20–30 minutes of light exercise or a walk daily to clear your head.
📝
Make a realistic timetable — 6–8 hours of study with regular breaks built in.
🧘
10 minutes of yoga or meditation each morning to start fresh.
⏱️ In the Exam Hall — 60-Second Reset
💡
When you feel nervous, do this:
1
Read the question paper calmly — don't rush.
2
Close your eyes and take 3 slow deep breaths.
3
Start with the questions you know best.
💬 Powerful Affirmations (Say These Every Morning!)
"I am calm and focused" "I have prepared well" "One question at a time" "This is just one exam"
