Q What is Design Thinking and how does it help in education?
A: Design Thinking is a problem-solving framework that solves complex problems keeping humans at the center. 
It simply means teaching the child to think like a problem-solver.
 Instead of just "ratta" (cramming), we teach them how to understand problems, think of different solutions, and choose the best one.
Example:
 If a child has difficulty in math, instead of just making them repeat formulas, we sit with them, understand where they are stuck, try different ways to explain, and find the method that suits them best.
 This method is used in top schools and universities around the world — even in places like Stanford University (USA).
Research says (source: Stanford Study) that children who learn using Design Thinking become more creative, more confident, and better at solving real-life problems.
Why it matters for your child:
They won’t just study for marks; they will study for understanding.
They will learn to think independently and take right decisions in life.
They will become fearless when facing exams, interviews, or challenges.
In short:
 Design Thinking opens the mind and prepares your child not just for school — but for life.
