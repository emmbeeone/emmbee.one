## Mathematics (8 Questions)

1. **Which of the following is a rational number?**  
   A) √3 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) π &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 3/5 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) None of these  
   **Answer:** C (3/5)  
   **Explanation:** A rational number can be expressed as a ratio of two integers. Among the options, 3/5 is already a fraction of integers, so it is rational. (√3 and π are well-known irrationals.)

2. **Solve for *x*: 5(x + 3) = 40.**  
   A) 4 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 5 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 6 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 7  
   **Answer:** B (x = 5)  
   **Explanation:** Divide both sides by 5: x + 3 = 8, so x = 8 – 3 = 5.

3. **Father’s age is 4 times his son’s age. After 4 years, father’s age will be 3 times the son’s age. What is the son’s present age?**  
   A) 5 years &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 8 years &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 10 years &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 12 years  
   **Answer:** B (8 years)  
   **Explanation:** Let son’s age = *s*. Then father’s age = 4*s*. After 4 years: 4*s* + 4 = 3*(s + 4). Solve: 4s + 4 = 3s + 12 ⇒ s = 8.

4. **15% of 200 is equal to:**  
   A) 25 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 30 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 35 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 40  
   **Answer:** B (30)  
   **Explanation:** 15% of 200 = (15/100)×200 = 30.

5. **The additive inverse of –3/5 is:**  
   A) 3/5 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) –3/5 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 5/3 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 0  
   **Answer:** A (3/5)  
   **Explanation:** The additive inverse of a number *a* is the number which when added to *a* gives 0. For –3/5, the inverse is +3/5.

6. **Which is greater: 4/9 or 1/2?**  
   A) 4/9 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 1/2 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Both equal &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Cannot say  
   **Answer:** B (1/2)  
   **Explanation:** Convert to decimals or common denominators: 4/9 ≈ 0.444 and 1/2 = 0.5, so 1/2 is larger.

7. **Solve for *x*: 3x – 4 = 11.**  
   A) 3 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 4 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 5 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 6  
   **Answer:** C (x = 5)  
   **Explanation:** Add 4 to both sides: 3x = 15, so x = 5.

8. **In a triangle, two angles are 50° and 60°. What is the third angle?**  
   A) 50° &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 60° &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 70° &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 80°  
   **Answer:** C (70°)  
   **Explanation:** The angles of a triangle sum to 180°. So the third angle = 180° – (50° + 60°) = 70°.

## Science (8 Questions)

1. **Which gas is released by green plants during photosynthesis?**  
   A) Oxygen &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Carbon dioxide &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Nitrogen &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Hydrogen  
   **Answer:** A (Oxygen)  
   **Explanation:** Green plants take in carbon dioxide and water, and produce carbohydrates and **oxygen** in photosynthesis【27†L457-L463】.

2. **Which of the following is an example of a chemical change?**  
   A) Boiling of water &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Rusting of iron &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Tearing of paper &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Melting of ice  
   **Answer:** B (Rusting of iron)  
   **Explanation:** Rusting of iron produces a new substance (iron oxide), so it is a chemical change. (Boiling, tearing, melting are physical changes.)

3. **Which material is a poor conductor of heat?**  
   A) Copper &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Iron &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Plastic &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Aluminum  
   **Answer:** C (Plastic)  
   **Explanation:** Metals (copper, iron, aluminum) are good conductors of heat; plastic is an insulator and conducts heat poorly.

4. **Which of the following will turn red litmus paper blue?**  
   A) Lemon juice (acidic) &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Vinegar (acidic) &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Baking soda solution (basic) &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Curd (acidic)  
   **Answer:** C (Baking soda solution)  
   **Explanation:** Baking soda solution is basic (alkaline), so it turns red litmus paper blue. (Acids keep red litmus red or turn blue litmus red.)

5. **Metals are good conductors of:**  
   A) Heat only &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Electricity only &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Both heat and electricity &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Neither  
   **Answer:** C (Both heat and electricity)  
   **Explanation:** Metals conduct both heat and electricity efficiently (this is a basic property of metals).

6. **In which surface can you see a clear image of yourself?**  
   A) A painted wall &nbsp;&nbsp; B) A plane mirror &nbsp;&nbsp; C) A wooden table &nbsp;&nbsp; D) A sheet of paper  
   **Answer:** B (A plane mirror)  
   **Explanation:** A smooth, shiny surface like a plane mirror reflects light to form a clear image of you.

7. **The SI unit of speed is:**  
   A) km/h &nbsp;&nbsp; B) m/s &nbsp;&nbsp; C) m/s² &nbsp;&nbsp; D) km  
   **Answer:** B (m/s)  
   **Explanation:** Speed = distance/time. In SI units, distance in meters (m) and time in seconds (s), so unit = m/s.

8. **Which gas do humans inhale (breathe in)?**  
   A) Oxygen &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Carbon dioxide &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Nitrogen &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Hydrogen  
   **Answer:** A (Oxygen)  
   **Explanation:** Humans breathe in oxygen, which is used by the body for respiration; carbon dioxide is exhaled.

## Social Science (6 Questions)

1. **Tropic of Cancer passes through how many Indian states?**  
   A) 6 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) 7 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) 8 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) 9  
   **Answer:** C (8 states)  
   **Explanation:** The Tropic of Cancer crosses **8** Indian states (Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Tripura, Mizoram)【72†L89-L94】.

2. **The Fundamental Right to Equality is guaranteed by which Article of the Indian Constitution?**  
   A) Article 14 &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Article 19 &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Article 21 &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Article 32  
   **Answer:** A (Article 14)  
   **Explanation:** Article 14 of the Constitution is titled “Equality before law” and guarantees the right to equality【53†L199-L202】.

3. **The Revolt of 1857 began in which city?**  
   A) Delhi &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Meerut &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Kanpur &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Lucknow  
   **Answer:** B (Meerut)  
   **Explanation:** The 1857 uprising started in Meerut on 10 May 1857 when sepoys mutinied【69†L140-L144】. (Meerut is widely considered the flashpoint of the revolt.)

4. **Which of the following is a renewable resource?**  
   A) Coal &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Wind energy &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Petroleum &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Natural gas  
   **Answer:** B (Wind energy)  
   **Explanation:** Wind energy (like solar energy) is naturally replenished and renewable. Coal, petroleum and natural gas are finite fossil fuels (non-renewable).

5. **Which is the largest state in India by area?**  
   A) Madhya Pradesh &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Rajasthan &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Maharashtra &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Uttar Pradesh  
   **Answer:** B (Rajasthan)  
   **Explanation:** Rajasthan is the largest state by land area in India【75†L186-L194】.

6. **Which of the following is not a state of India?**  
   A) Bihar &nbsp;&nbsp; B) Assam &nbsp;&nbsp; C) Sikkim &nbsp;&nbsp; D) Nepal  
   **Answer:** D (Nepal)  
   **Explanation:** Nepal is a separate country, not a state of India.
